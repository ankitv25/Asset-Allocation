The six-sleeve architecture and the research chain

The six levers are fixed. The lever weights set the portfolio's character and are decided first; instruments live inside their parent lever as implementation. The research in §D follows a fixed chain for each sleeve, so every allocation is traceable from portfolio role through to a draft weight.

existing sleeve → portfolio role → institutional evidence → realistic min/max range → internal split → ETF/proxy → CMA input logic → MRS linkage → constraint → draft validation weight

D3 · Real estate / REITs

Endowment 9%Accelerated base 8%range 6–12%

Real estate is a distinct real-asset lever, kept visible rather than folded into equity. It is equity-like in beta but driven by rents, occupancy, cap rates, and real yields, and it carries an income and inflation-sensitivity profile equity lacks. Swensen sized it as a separate ~20% lever; core institutional real estate runs nearer 8–12%. JPM's core real-estate CMA is high (8.2%), reflecting private/core entry yields; listed REITs are drafted more conservatively given their higher equity beta and liquidity.

D4 · Commodities

Endowment 4%Accelerated base 5%range 2–8%

Commodities are a separate inflation/real-asset lever with a different economic role from real estate. They are close to the only liquid asset that tends to rise when equities and bonds fall together, as in 2022, which is why they are not merged into a generic "real assets" bucket with rate-sensitive REITs. The sleeve is modest in calm regimes and scales up when the inflation pillar fires (§G). Forward return is moderate; the value is the inflation-shock hedge and the low-to-moderate equity correlation.

D5 · Alternatives / trend

Endowment 6%Accelerated base 8%range 4–16%

Trend and managed futures are a diversifying strategy sleeve, valued for a near-zero equity correlation and a tendency to be convex in sustained drawdowns — they have historically paid off in exactly the prolonged crises where bonds disappoint. The architecture is honest about the role: this sleeve diversifies the growth book; it does not replace it. It is sized modestly at neutral and scaled up as growth steps down, capped so it never substitutes for the return engine.

F

Covariance and optimisation logic

Two estimation decisions carry over and are unchanged. Correlations are estimated from full pairwise long-term history (the 50/50 long-versus-recent blend was dropped after a sweep showed monotonic improvement toward full history); regime-conditional covariance was retracted because forward-probability weighting underweights stress regimes — regime information lives in the tactical overlay (§G), not the strategic covariance. The matrix is nearest-PSD-guarded.

StepRule
Correlation estimationFull pairwise long-term history; nearest-PSD guard
Views (Black-Litterman)Excess-return space; equilibrium prior from the benchmark blend only
ObjectiveMaximise expected geometric return s.t. volatility and drawdown budget (not max-Sharpe)
Optimiser scopeImplements within the sleeve ranges of §D; it does not set top-level character
Regime conditioningTactical overlay only, never inside strategic covariance
G

MRS regime linkage (five pillars)

The Accelerated book's dynamism runs through the locked MRS v2.1 and its five pillars — Growth, Inflation, Liquidity/Rates, Credit, and Market Stress. Each pillar maps to specific sleeves and proxies with an explicit tilt and a draft weight impact. The strategic base is regime-agnostic; the overlay reads the pillars and tilts around it. Crucially, the kind of defense is pillar-specific: a growth-led contraction calls for government duration, an inflation-led one calls for TIPS and commodities, and a credit-led one cuts the IG tilt — the pillars distinguish which.

H

Implementation constraints

Constraints prevent the failure modes of the over-defensive v3.0. The binding one is the investment-grade cap, which keeps IG a conditional internal tilt and makes a 30%-style credit block structurally impossible. Growth floors keep both books from drifting into dead capital; a diversifier cap keeps trend from substituting for the growth engine.

Decision register and pipeline hand-off

Settled

Six-lever architecture with instruments nested inside; IG an internal fixed-income tilt, never standalone or dominant; REITs and commodities as separate levers; geometric-growth objective under a drawdown budget; full pairwise long-term covariance with regime-conditional covariance retracted; Black-Litterman views in excess-return space with the prior from the benchmark blend only; two portfolios — a more aggressive Endowment and a dynamic Accelerated SAA/DAA; MRS five-pillar overlay driving the accelerated tilts.

Draft, to be finalised by the pipeline

All capital-market assumptions (anchored to JPM 2026 here); the new-sleeve assumptions (trend, listed REITs, commodities, TIPS); the internal US/international/EM equity split; calibrated constraint bounds; and all sleeve weights. The production data migration (CRSP/WRDS) keeps every number provisional until complete.

Hand-off sequence

The pipeline ingests production data, finalises the CMAs and the long-term covariance, optimises within the §D ranges under the geometric objective and the §H constraints, applies the §G MRS overlay, and runs the §I validation gates to accept, reject, or revise. This document supplies the research, the logic, and the draft magnitudes; the pipeline supplies the final, validated numbers.
